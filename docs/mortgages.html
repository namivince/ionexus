Mortgage Tips

Loan-to-Value (LTV) Ratio

For expatriates, banks in Dubai typically offer up to 75% LTV for properties under AED 5 million, and 65% for properties above that. UAE nationals can get up to 80% LTV. This means you'll need a minimum down payment of 20-35% depending on your residency status and property value.

Documentation Requirements

Be prepared with your passport, visa, Emirates ID, salary certificate, bank statements (6 months), and credit report. Self-employed individuals will need trade license and audited financial statements. Having these documents ready can speed up your mortgage approval process.

Additional Costs

Beyond the down payment, budget for Dubai Land Department fee (4%), registration fee (0.25%), bank processing fee (1%), valuation fee (AED 2,500-3,500), and mortgage registration (0.25%). These additional costs typically add up to around 7-8% of the property value.
